The Web Conference in Scotland

The inaugural Whisky Web conference kicks off in Edinburgh on the 13th and 14th of April 2012. A web conference created for the web community, by the web community; Whisky Web will have something to offer everyone who works with the web, be they a designer, a developer or something in between. This is an amazing opportunity to get your geek on in Scotland's inspiring capital.

Information

What is Whisky Web?

Whisky Web was born from the absence of readily accessible web conferences north-of-the-border. While there are a great many web-related conferences throughout the UK, few are held in Scotland. In recent years we've seen The Highland Fling and the Turing Festival put Edinburgh on the conference map, and Whisky Web hopes to capitlise on their success in bringing more exciting and awesome talks to a new Scottish web conference.

Whisky Web will cover a broad range of subject areas, with a key focus on what makes the web great. We'll be language agnostic; we won't focus on a particular development paradigm, nor will we concentrate on front-end design over back-end coding. We want to be inclusive, and while it's very difficult to be all things to all people, we hope to have a good shot at it!

You don't need to like a dram of whisky, (but it might help!) and you don't need to know your haggis, neeps and tatties from your cock-a-leekie – if you care about the web and want to hear awesome speakers wax lyrical about topics close to their hearts, and yours, get a ticket and get to Scotland this Spring!

Check Out the Full Event Schedule

Spread over two days, Whisky Web will combine a more traditional two-track programme of talks the first day, with a hackathon event on day two. But the fun doesn't begin there; the evening before the conference the organisers would like to invite the delegates to join them in Edinburgh's Brewdog bar for an informal opportunity to become better acquainted.

How to get to the Conference

The Hub is situated on Castlehill, adjacent to Edinburgh Castle in the very heart of Edinburgh's historic Old Town. It is less than half a mile from Waverly train station and 7 miles from Edinburgh International Airport.

Paid on street and disabled parking is available on Johnston Terrace and there is an NCP car park located on Castle Terrace which is a short walk from The Hub.
